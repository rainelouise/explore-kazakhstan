Kazakh Culture & Traditions

Kazakh Traditions & Customs

Kazakhstan is known for its warm hospitality. Guests are treated with great respect and offered traditional food. Kazakhs wear ornate embroidered garments, and celebrations are filled with music, dance, and feasting.

Famous Kazakh Delicacies

Beshbarmak

The national dish of Kazakhstan, made of boiled meat and wide noodles, usually served with broth.

Kumis

A fermented mare’s milk drink with a slightly sour taste, popular among Kazakh nomads.

Baursak

Small, fried dough balls, similar to doughnuts, often served with honey or sugar.

Festivals & Events

Nowruz (Persian New Year)

Celebrated on March 21, Nowruz marks the arrival of spring with feasting, music, and games.

Kóshpendiler Álemi (World of Nomads)

A festival showcasing traditional Kazakh horse games, archery, and wrestling.
