About Kazakhstan

Basic Information

Kazakhstan, the worldâ€™s largest landlocked country, is situated in Central Asia and partly in Europe. It covers an area of 2.7 million square kilometers, making it the ninth-largest country in the world.

The nation is known for its diverse landscapes, from vast steppes to towering mountains. It has a population of approximately 19 million, with major cities including Almaty and the capital, Astana.

Languages Spoken

Kazakh is the official language of Kazakhstan and is widely spoken across the country. It belongs to the Turkic language family and has evolved significantly over time.

Russian is also widely spoken, especially in business and government. In urban areas, English is becoming more common, particularly among the younger population.

History & Cultural Highlights

Kazakhstan has a rich nomadic heritage. For centuries, Kazakh tribes roamed the steppes, relying on livestock and trade for survival. The region was later influenced by the Mongol Empire, Russian expansion, and Soviet rule.

Today, Kazakhstan preserves its traditions through music, dance, and celebrations such as Nauryz. Iconic landmarks include the Baiterek Tower in Astana and the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
